The Why

"Success isn't about how much money you make. It's about the difference you make in people's lives" ~ Michelle Obama

As a college student, I obsessed over having a great "elevator pitch". It was a useful skill to learn, but it only showed the what - what I've done, what I'm doing, and what I will do. The why is immensely more important, because it guides all of my decisions about where and how I choose to spend my time. And my time, in the end, is my most valuable resource and my greatest tool to influence the things I care about.

Throughout my life, there have been pivotal people that either helped me find my path, or layed the foundational bricks for me to walk the path myself. And my goal is to do the same as much as I can throughout my life.

Developer

Technology is really cool and fun, and I enjoy building out awesome solutions for users. At the same time, I focus on how to make it accessible for anyone to learn, enjoy doing, and succeed in a career.

Speaker

Actions speak louder than words...unless someone isn't seeing your actions. Speaking gives me a platform to share ideas, inspire people, and give light to work I care about.

Leader

Building the world I want to live in one step at a time. Whether a software world with consistent coding standards and engineering fundamentals, or an inclusive and diverse work culture. It goes both ways.
